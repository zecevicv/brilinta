  • ADHESION

    Plaque rupture signals nearby platelets to adhere to the injury site. Adhesion triggers platelet activation.

  • ACTIVATION

    Activated platelets change shape and express recptors that release the potent platelet agonist ADP, leading to initial thrombus formation.

    BRILINTA PREVENTS ADP FROM BINDING

  • AGGREGATION

    Activated platelets aggregate at the active lesion site, leading to growth of thrombus.

PLATO trial design​

n=18,624 patients with ACS (UA, NSTEMI or STEMI) randomized within 24 hours of symptom onset to BRILINTA or clopidogrel

BRILINTA 90mg (n=9,333)​

180mg loading dose​
Followed by 90mg bid + ASA 75-100mg daily ​​

Primary efficacy endpoint: ​​

Composite of CV death, MI (excluding silent MI) or stroke ​​

Secondary endpoints:​ ​​

  • CV death + MI + stroke in patients intended for invasive management​
  • All-cause-death + MI + stroke​
  • CV death + MI + stroke + severe recurrent cardiac ischemia + recurrent cardiac ischemia + transient ischemic attack + other arterial thrombotic events​
  • CV death alone/MI alone/stroke alone/all-cause-death alone

Initial treatment approaches​
28% planned non-invasive management (n=5,216)
72% planned invasive management (n=13,408)​

Clopidogrel 90mg (n=9,291)​​

300-600mg loading dose*​
Followed by 75mg daily + ASA 75-100mg daily​ ​​

Primary safety endpoint: ​​

Total PLATO major bleeding ​​

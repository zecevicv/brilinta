The central role of platelets in thrombus formation​

The pathophysiology of a thrombus includes atherosclerotic plaque rupture, platelet adhesion, activation, and aggregation leading to various degrees of thrombosis and occlusion, and ultimately, myocardial infarction or stroke.

    Plaque rupture signals nearby platelets to adhere to the injury site. Adhesion triggers platelet activation.

  • ACTIVATION
